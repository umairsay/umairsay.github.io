Economics · Sports Analytics 2024 – 25

Visible Value: Wage Determination in MLS

MLS doesn't pay defenders. Across every position, better defensive output is associated with lower wages. Attack and progression are rewarded. Defense is invisible to the market.

440 outfield players, 2024 MLS season. FBref performance data, MLSPA salaries, Google Trends popularity. Fixed-effects panel regressions with clustered standard errors.

Salary by roster designation

Label precedes labor. Designated Players earn ~4× a regular roster player before a single action is counted.

Performance components vs salary

Four components slope up. One slopes down.

Defense score vs salary — by position

Five positions. Five negative slopes. No exceptions. Defend more, earn less(?!)

Overall performance vs salary

Messi top right, Afrifa bottom right. Busquets top left. The gap between the line and where players sit is the market's mispricing.

Popularity vs salary — by designation

Fame pays — but only for Designated Players. For everyone else, more Google searches barely moves the needle.

Marginal effect of fame across performance levels

Below-average performer? Being popular hurts your wage. Above-average? Fame amplifies it. The market pays for celebrity on top of output — not instead of it.

Club wage premium / discount — relative to league average

Red Bulls and Inter Miami pay the most above market. Houston and Charlotte the most below. Controlling for who they signed — this is front-office philosophy, made visible.
